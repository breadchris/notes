take notes to understand your language

February 28, 2024 · 2 min · breadchris

There probably wasn’t one person who looked up and said, “Oh well, that is obviously the ‘sky’.” Even if they did, how did that word become so prolific in the English language? “Sky” was written and rewritten, whispered and proclaimed throughout history, eventually becoming ingrained in everyday speech. It is believed that the word “sky” has a Germanic origin, traceable to the Old English word “sceo” or “sceoh,” which meant “cloud” or “cover.” Over time, this evolved into the word “sky” that we use today.

As humans began to look up at the vast expanse above them, they needed a way to describe it, and “sky” became the perfect word to encapsulate the endless blue above. With the growth of language and communication, the word spread and became widely used in various cultures and languages.

Etymology is the study of how words come to be, and at best we have rough approximations of their lineage that span centuries.

Taking notes is essentially a form of language in itself. When we jot down key points or important information in a concise and organized manner, we are essentially creating a shorthand version of the original message. Using acronyms or abbreviations in our notes is a way to condense information even further and make it easier to reference and understand later on.

For example, in a lecture or meeting, taking notes using abbreviations like “CRM” for Content Resource Management or “ROI” for Return on Investment allows us to quickly capture the main ideas without having to write out the entire phrase each time. These acronyms function as a form of language that conveys specific meanings to us and others who are familiar with them.

Additionally, note-taking often involves organizing information into categories or hierarchies, similar to how language functions with grammar rules and sentence structures. By categorizing information and connecting related points in our notes, we are creating a coherent narrative that helps us better understand and retain the information being presented.

By taking notes and writing down what you see around you, maybe there is a word that starts to emerge. An undeniable thing or pattern that you have to come up with a word to describe in your notes. A unifying concept that eventually is something you share with others and explain to them what you are seeing.
